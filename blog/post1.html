Building a Brain-Controlled Prosthetic

Exploring how EEG technology can transform robotics and accessibility

EEG technology allows us to interpret brain signals to control devices like prosthetics. My project focuses on making this accessible and affordable, using Python and Arduino.

The process involves capturing brain waves, processing them in real-time, and translating them into movements. Challenges include signal noise and calibration, but the potential for rehabilitation is huge.
